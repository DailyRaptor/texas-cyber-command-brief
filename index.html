Establishing Texas Cyber Command • Interactive Business Brief

Executive Summary

Texas House Bill 150 (HB 150) delivers a historic upgrade to Texas' cybersecurity strategy. It establishes the Texas Cyber Command, a dedicated state agency focused on protecting government systems, critical infrastructure, and local communities.

For public sector leaders and industry partners, this legislation creates a centralized cybersecurity authority, streamlined contracting pathways, expanded incident response capabilities, and a robust roadmap for improving resilience across the state.

Budget & Funding Details

×

Initial Budget: $200M+ (2025-2027)

The Texas Cyber Command will be funded through multiple revenue streams to ensure sustainable operations and growth.

Funding Sources:

  • State Appropriations: Primary funding through legislative budget allocation
  • Cost Recovery Services: Fees for technical assistance and specialized services
  • Federal Grants: Cybersecurity infrastructure and workforce development grants
  • Private Sector Partnerships: Joint funding for specific initiatives and programs
  • Gifts and Donations: Authority to accept gifts, grants, and donations from various sources

Budget Flexibility: The Command has authority to hold patents, copyrights, and licensing agreements, creating additional revenue opportunities through vendor collaborations and intellectual property development.

Staffing & Organization Plan

×

Phased Hiring Approach

Phase 1: Initial Launch (150-200 employees)

  • Location: Majority based in San Antonio with secure SCIF facility
  • Focus: Core operations, leadership team, and essential cybersecurity functions
  • Timeline: September 2025 - December 2026

Phase 2: Full Expansion (300-400 employees statewide)

  • Distribution: Statewide deployment through regional centers
  • Growth Areas: Digital forensics, threat intelligence, training programs
  • Timeline: 2027 and beyond

Leadership Structure:

  • Chief Executive: Governor-appointed, Senate-confirmed, two-year terms
  • Specialized Directors: Threat Intelligence, Incident Response, Digital Forensics
  • DIR Staff Transition: Existing cybersecurity teams, contracts, and systems transfer to Command

Immediate Actions:

Texas Cyber Command: Independent Cybersecurity Authority

Agency Structure & Authority:

  • Independent Operations: Dedicated agency focused solely on cybersecurity
  • Governor Oversight: Chief Executive appointed by Governor, confirmed by Senate
  • Procurement Independence: Authority to make cybersecurity purchases and contracts
  • Emergency Powers: Special authorities during cybersecurity incidents
  • Property Rights: Can hold patents, copyrights, and licensing agreements

Business Opportunities:

  • Direct contracting with dedicated cybersecurity agency
  • Specialized procurement processes for cybersecurity solutions
  • Partnership opportunities for intellectual property development
  • Access to emergency procurement during cyber incidents
  • Long-term strategic partnerships for cybersecurity infrastructure

Competitive Advantage: Working with a specialized cybersecurity agency means faster decision-making, deeper technical expertise, and more strategic long-term planning for cybersecurity investments.

Emergency Purchasing Authority

×

Rapid Response Procurement Powers

Emergency Authority Details:

  • Bypass Standard Procurement: Exempts emergency cybersecurity purchases from normal state procurement rules
  • Immediate Response: Enables acquisition of critical tools and services within hours, not months
  • Vendor Direct Engagement: Direct contracting with cybersecurity specialists during emergencies
  • Cost Recovery: Authority to charge for emergency response services provided

Scenarios for Emergency Procurement:

  • Active cyber attacks on state infrastructure
  • Critical vulnerabilities requiring immediate patching
  • Incident response requiring specialized forensic tools
  • Threat intelligence requiring immediate action
  • Recovery operations following major cyber incidents

Vendor Benefits: Pre-qualified vendors can respond immediately to emergency situations, creating revenue opportunities and demonstrating value during critical moments.
